## Purpose
To prevent foodborne illness by ensuring that sick employees do not contaminate food or food-contact surfaces.

## Employee Responsibility
All employees must report the following symptoms to the Person in Charge (PIC) before starting work or immediately if symptoms develop during a shift:
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Fever
- Sore throat with fever
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)

Employees must also report diagnoses of:
- Norovirus
- Hepatitis A
- Salmonella
- Shigella
- E. coli

Exclusion & Restriction
- Employees experiencing vomiting or diarrhea will be excluded from the facility
- Employees with sore throat and fever will be restricted from food handling
- Employees diagnosed with a foodborne illness will follow health department guidance before returning to work

Return to Work
- Employees may return to work after symptoms have resolved for at least 24 hours or with medical clearance, when required
- Final approval for return to work is determined by the PIC

Responsibility
- Employees must self-report symptoms and diagnoses
- PIC is responsible for enforcing this policy and maintaining compliance
